# Mason's Engineering Challenges

Work through these challenges to level up your engineering skills. Each one teaches you something real engineers use every day. Copy the challenge text right into your AI Lab and watch it build!

---

## LEVEL 1: Apprentice Engineer

These get you comfortable with how the lab works.

### Challenge 1.1 — Ball Drop
> "Build a simulation where I click to drop a ball. It should fall with gravity and bounce when it hits the floor. Add a slider to change gravity strength."

**What you'll learn:** Physics simulation, gravity, collision detection

### Challenge 1.2 — Color Mixer
> "Create a color mixing tool with three sliders for Red, Green, and Blue (0-255). Show the mixed color in a big square, and display the hex code."

**What you'll learn:** RGB color model, user input, real-time updates

### Challenge 1.3 — Countdown Timer
> "Build a countdown timer where I type in a number of seconds, press Start, and it counts down with a big animated display. Play a sound when it hits zero."

**What you'll learn:** Time management, state machines, event handling

### Challenge 1.4 — Simple Calculator
> "Make a calculator that can do +, -, x, and /. It should look like a real calculator with buttons and a display screen."

**What you'll learn:** User interface design, math operations, state management

## LEVEL 2: Junior Engineer

Now you're building things that actually simulate real-world systems.

### Challenge 2.1 — Pendulum Lab
> "Create a pendulum simulation. I should be able to drag the bob to any angle and release it. Add sliders for string length, gravity, and damping. Show the period in real time."

**What you'll learn:** Simple harmonic motion, angular physics, energy conservation

### Challenge 2.2 — Beam Stress Tester
> "Build a beam stress simulator: a horizontal beam supported at both ends. I can place weights anywhere on it. Show how the beam bends and color-code the stress (green=safe, yellow=warning, red=danger)."

**What you'll learn:** Structural engineering, load distribution, material stress

### Challenge 2.3 — Traffic Light Controller
> "Create a traffic intersection with 4 traffic lights. Build a state machine that cycles them through green/yellow/red safely. Add a button for a pedestrian crossing signal."

**What you'll learn:** State machines, timing, safety systems (this is real engineering!)

### Challenge 2.4 — Pulley System
> "Build a pulley system simulator. Let me add up to 4 pulleys and see how they reduce the force needed to lift a weight. Show the mechanical advantage and trade-off with rope length."

**What you'll learn:** Mechanical advantage, simple machines, force/distance trade-offs

---

## LEVEL 3: Engineer

These are real engineering problems. You'll need to think carefully about how to describe what you want.

### Challenge 3.1 — Bridge Builder
> "Create a bridge builder game. I click to place nodes, then connect them with beams. Add a 'test' button that applies weight and shows which beams are under tension (blue) and compression (red). Beams that are overstressed should snap."

**What you'll learn:** Truss analysis, tension vs compression, structural failure

### Challenge 3.2 — Projectile Launcher
> "Build a projectile launcher. I set the angle (0-90 degrees) and power (1-100). When I launch, show the projectile arc with a dotted trail. Display max height, distance traveled, and flight time. Add wind resistance as an option."

**What you'll learn:** Projectile motion, drag, optimization

### Challenge 3.3 — Gear Box Designer
> "Make a gearbox designer where I chain gears together. Each gear has a tooth count I can adjust. Show the output RPM, torque multiplier, and direction. Animate the gears meshing and spinning."

**What you'll learn:** Gear ratios, mechanical power transmission, RPM/torque trade-offs

### Challenge 3.4 — Water Flow Network
> "Create a pipe network simulator. I place pipes, pumps, valves, and tanks. Water flows through based on pressure. Valves can open and close. Show flow rate and pressure at each point."

**What you'll learn:** Fluid dynamics basics, network flow, pressure systems

## LEVEL 4: Senior Engineer

These require you to combine multiple engineering concepts. Give the AI detailed instructions — you're the lead engineer now.

### Challenge 4.1 — Robot Arm
> "Build a 3-joint robot arm. Each joint has a slider (-180 to 180 degrees). The end effector (gripper) should follow smoothly. Add objects on a table that the arm can reach. Display the x,y coordinates of the gripper. Bonus: add an 'auto-reach' mode where I click a target and the arm figures out the joint angles."

**What you'll learn:** Forward/inverse kinematics, robotics, coordinate systems

### Challenge 4.2 — Circuit Lab
> "Create a circuit simulator where I can place batteries, resistors, LEDs, switches, and capacitors on a grid. Wire them together by dragging. Calculate and display voltage, current, and power at each component using Ohm's law. LEDs should light up when enough current flows."

**What you'll learn:** Ohm's law, circuit analysis, electrical engineering fundamentals

### Challenge 4.3 — Elevator System
> "Design an elevator control system for a 10-floor building with 2 elevators. People appear on random floors and press up/down. The system should efficiently dispatch elevators. Show a real-time visualization and track stats: average wait time, total trips, energy used."

**What you'll learn:** Scheduling algorithms, optimization, system design

### Challenge 4.4 — Solar System
> "Build an accurate solar system model with all 8 planets. Use real relative sizes and orbital periods. I can zoom in/out and speed up/slow down time. Click a planet to see facts. Show orbital paths and current positions."

**What you'll learn:** Orbital mechanics, scale, gravitational systems

## LEVEL 5: Master Engineer

These are open-ended design challenges. There's no single right answer — it's about YOUR creative engineering solution.

### Challenge 5.1 — Design a City
> Design a city builder where you place roads, buildings, power plants, and water systems. Each building needs power and water to function. Challenge: build a city for 10,000 people using the least amount of infrastructure.

### Challenge 5.2 — Rube Goldberg Machine
> Build a virtual Rube Goldberg machine — a chain reaction where one thing triggers the next. Include at least 8 steps: dominoes, ramps, balls, levers, catapults, etc. The final step should ring a bell.

### Challenge 5.3 — Mars Habitat
> Design a Mars habitat. You have limited materials and need to provide: air, water recycling, power (solar), food growing, and shelter from radiation. Display resource levels and survival time.

### Challenge 5.4 — Your Invention
> Think of something that doesn't exist yet. Describe it to the AI and build a working prototype. This is YOUR invention. Document it in your Lab Notebook!

## How to Use These Challenges

1. Open Mason's Engineering Laboratory
2. Copy the quoted text from any challenge
3. Paste it into the chat
4. Watch the AI build it
5. Then iterate! Say things like:
   - "Make the gravity stronger"
   - "Add a reset button"
   - "Show the math behind the simulation"
   - "Make it look more realistic"
   - "Can you explain how the code works?"

**Pro tip:** After the AI builds something, ask it "Can you explain the physics/engineering behind this?" — you'll learn way more than just the code!
